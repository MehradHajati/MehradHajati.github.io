Research Assistant

I did research with Professor Ralf Bruening at Mount Allison University during the summers of 2022 and 2023. During the first summer, my main responsibility was to solve a geometric problem between two different data sets from Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM) and Electron Backscatter Diffraction (EBSD), for which I created "JCube". I created JCube using the AWT and Swing packages in Java with an MVC structure. Using R, I was able to visualize the data and present them to the research investors. During the second summer, I started coding in C to visualize and tilt a surface according to given parameters. I presented my research during the Atlantic Undergraduate Physics and Astronomy Conference at Saint Mary's University; and again during the Canadian Undergraduate Physics Conference at Waterloo University for professors and students.
