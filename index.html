Enigma Machine

One of my favorite courses during my bachelors degree was Cryptography. I found classical ciphers simple yet effective and was amazed how modern ciphers draw ideas from them to protect our data. I decided to follow my passion for classical ciphers and created the “Enigma Machine”, using the AWT and Swing packages. The Enigma Machine is a graphic user interface that explains how classical ciphers work and allows the user to enter ciphertexts or plaintexts along with appropriate keys to decrypt or encrypt them. Currently, it can encrypt and decrypt eleven ciphers. I decided on an MVC structure with this project as I found it intuitive to use and implement. The source code can be found in this repository:

Research Assistant

I did research with Professor Ralf Bruening at Mount Allison University during the summers of 2022 and 2023. During the first summer, my main responsibility was to solve a geometric problem between two different data sets from Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM) and Electron Backscatter Diffraction (EBSD), for which I created "JCube". I created JCube using the AWT and Swing packages in Java with an MVC structure. Using R, I was able to visualize the data and present them to the research investors. During the second summer, I started coding in C to visualize and tilt the AFM surface according to given parameters. This research ultimately lead to my honours project which will be completed by April of 2024. I presented my research during the Atlantic Undergraduate Physics and Astronomy Conference at Saint Mary's University; and again during the Canadian Undergraduate Physics Conference at Waterloo University for professors and students.
